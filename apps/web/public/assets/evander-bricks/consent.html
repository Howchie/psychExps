Invitation

You are invited to participate in a research study conducted by the University of Western Australia (UWA). You are invited to take part in this project because you are a member of Prolific’s participant pool. Participation is voluntary, and this document will provide you with all the necessary details to help you make an informed decision about whether to participate.

Aim of the Study (What is the project about?)

The aim of the study is to understand how we work to complete multiple tasks under time constraints.

What does participation involve?

If you decide to participate in this study, you will be asked to complete a cognitive psychology experiment. The duration of the entire experiment will be approximately 1 hour

You will be asked to complete a computer-based task at a time convenient to you. You will be given multiple tasks to complete, such as collecting a certain number of counters, completing imaginary buildings in a game, or clearing rows of boxes. You will be asked to carry out activities, such as estimating time intervals, solving simple mathematics problems, or making decisions between options, to complete the tasks within a specified time.

Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal from the Study

Participation in this study is entirely voluntary, and does not prejudice any right to compensation, which you may be entitled to under statute or common law. At any time before or during the experiment, you may, without prejudice, withdraw from the study without reason or justification. Withdrawing from the study will not result in any consequences nor will your treatment by the researchers or academic staff be affected in any way.

Your data will be stored in a non-identified fashion, meaning that no identifying information will be associated with the data we collect from you in the experiment. Accordingly, you will not be able to withdraw your consent for us to use your data once the study has been completed.

Your privacy

All data you provide will be anonymous. Your identifying information will not be linked to your responses, and no personal data will be collected that could identify you. Your participation in this study and any information you provide will be treated in a confidential manner.

All electronic data will be stored on a Microsoft Teams/Sharepoint for the project, and this will be accessible only to the researchers named at the start of this document. On completion of the research, anonymised data will be stored on an institutional server for a period of 7 years, or you have reached the age of 25, whichever is later. Information from this project will be published but your name and identifying details will not be used in any publication arising out of the research without your consent.

On publication of this work, your anonymized data (without any personally identifying information) will be made publicly and permanently available by banking the data from the entire study on data sharing websites, particularly the Open Science Framework (https://osf.io). This will allow any researchers (including ourselves) to potentially re-use these data by running new analyses to generate new findings, and will allow other researchers to verify our published analyses.

If you wish to access the results of the study once they are available, please contact the lead researcher Prof Simon Farrell (simon.farrell@uwa.edu.au).

Possible Benefits

This study will help researchers lean more about how we work towards multiple goals and balance different tasks. There may not be a direct benefit to you beyond any monetary reimbursement as described in the advertisement.

Possible Risks and Risk Management Plan

The risks of the study are minimal. Nevertheless, it is possible that some participants may experience fatigue across the different tasks in the experiment. You will have the opportunity to take short breaks between each task throughout the experiment. However, if you find yourself uncomfortable with fatigue or by anything else during the experiment, or if you wish to terminate at any point for any reason, you may discontinue the experiment at any time without prejudice.

Reimbursement
